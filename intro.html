What does git do?

git stores a set of snapshots of the entire history of your project

What does git do?

All of your changes, throughout the entire development process are saved locally to your machine.

You can restore them at any time, and if you break something you can undo it easily

These "snapshots" are called commits

The set of all commits makes up the history of your project, and is called the repository, or repo

When you go to github, you're browsing other people's repos
